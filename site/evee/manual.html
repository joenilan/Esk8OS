Manual

Install, wire, ride.

EVEE is a telemetry computer for VESC-based vehicles. It plugs into your ESC's COMM port, reads it a dozen times a second, and turns that into a live dashboard, honest battery math, ride logs, and a phone app. It is read-only by design — it can never command your motors, so a dashboard fault can't become a ride fault.

Two ways in: buy a preloaded board and just build the cable, or DIY everything — the firmware is open source and flashes onto off-the-shelf ESP32-S3 hardware.

Hardware

One firmware, three kinds of glass.

Every tier runs the same firmware and the same app. Less display simply moves more of the picture to your phone.

Full display

LilyGo T-Display-S3

1.9" color dashboard with two buttons: eight pages, four HUD faces, eight themes, on-board settings. The flagship build.

Mini display

ESP32-S3 + 0.91" OLED

A 128×32 glance display: speed, battery, volts, range, alerts, pairing code and service screens. Your phone is the buttons — the app flips its faces remotely.

Headless

bare ESP32-S3

No screen at all. The board rides hidden in the enclosure; the app is the entire dashboard. Cheapest way onto any vehicle.

Wiring

Four wires to the VESC.

EVEE talks to the ESC over UART through the VESC's COMM port and is powered by it. Same pinout on every tier.

VESC COMM 5V→ EVEE 5V — the ESC powers the board
VESC COMM GND→ EVEE GND
VESC COMM TX→ EVEE GPIO 18 (board RX)
VESC COMM RX→ EVEE GPIO 17 (board TX)

TX crosses to RX — if the link stays down, swapping 17/18 is the first thing to try. Most VESCs use a JST-PH 2.0 COMM connector; check your ESC's pin label order before crimping.

VESC Tool settings

once

App Settings → General → App to Use: PPM and UART (keeps a PPM remote working) or UART. Baud 115200. Dual motors need CAN status messages on — EVEE finds the second ESC by itself.

Adding the 0.91" OLED

mini tier

SSD1306 128×32 I²C module, four wires: VCC→3.3V · GND→GND · SDA→GPIO 8 · SCL→GPIO 9. Address 0x3C (the usual default). Flash the OLED build and it just shows up.

⚠ Power caution

read this

Never connect USB while the board is wired to a powered vehicle. Bench with USB only (ESC unpowered), ride with vehicle power only. One 5V source at a time.

USB

Which port is which.

T-Display-S3

One USB-C. That's the one.

ESP32-S3 dev boards

Most have two USB-C ports. EVEE uses the native USB port — the one labeled USB (on the units we ship, the right-hand port with the connector facing you). Flashing, the serial console, and log downloads all live there. The port labeled COM/UART is a serial bridge EVEE doesn't use.

DIY first flash

open source

Clone the repo, install PlatformIO, plug the native USB port and run pio run -e <your board> -t upload. Environments: tdisplay_s3_ride_release · esp32s3_oled_i2c_usb · esp32s3_headless_usb. Preloaded boards skip this entirely.

First boot

It configures itself from your ESC.

EVEE ships knowing nothing about your vehicle — and that's the feature. On first link it reads your VESC's own configuration (cell count, pack capacity, cutoff voltages, motor poles, gearing, wheel size) and uses it as ground truth. Anything you change later is kept as your override on top; the app's settings page shows exactly which is which.

01

Power it

Wired to the ESC, turn the vehicle on. Displays show a boot bar, then live data — or NO VESC with your pairing code while unlinked. Demo mode (simulated data, marked DEMO) is available for bench play.

02

Pair the app

Install the ESK8OS app, scan, and match the 4-character pair code shown on the board (screen, screensaver, or the scan list itself). Telemetry starts streaming immediately.

03

Tune what's yours

Wheel size is the classic override — measure your real rolling diameter and set it in the app; GPS in the trip recorder will confirm it. Battery floors, units, themes and rider name live there too.

Buttonless boards navigate from the app: double-tap the dashboard → Controls → PREV FACE / NEXT FACE.

Riding

What it's doing back there.

Honest battery math

Sag-compensated charge, learned pack resistance and real deliverable energy — range that predicts the loaded cutoff, not the resting one.

Black-box logging

One CSV per session on the board: volts, amps, temps, faults, per-motor split — with link-loss and voltage-collapse events flushed the moment they happen.

Ride recording

The app maps every ride with the board as the source of truth for speed and distance, GPS as the fallback, and full playback with per-second telemetry.

VESC Tool passthrough

Bridge mode turns EVEE into a wireless VESC Tool link (BLE or WiFi) — configure your ESC without pulling the enclosure.

Updates

Over the air, from the app.

01

Start export

App → Controls → EXPORT / OTA. The board raises its own WiFi network — name and per-device password appear on its screen and in the app.

02

Join it

Connect your phone to that network and open http://192.168.4.1 — ride logs to download, firmware update form below.

03

Flash

Upload the build for your hardware from the release feed (checksums published). The board verifies, reboots, and keeps your odometer and settings.

For tinkerers

The serial console.

Plug the native USB port into any serial terminal at 115200 baud and type help. Highlights:

stat · diaglive telemetry · VESC link diagnostics down to the wire counters
cfgevery setting with its source — your override, VESC-read, or default
vstatthe ESC's own accumulated ride stats (FW6+)
trip · odo · caltrip, lifetime odometer, learned battery calibration
logs · cat · mcconfsession CSVs and the raw VESC config capture
demo on|offsimulated telemetry for bench testing

Safety, restated: the firmware's only write to the ESC is the odometer. No current control, no duty control, no cutoffs — your VESC and remote remain the only things that drive the motors. EVEE is instrumentation. Ride within your limits; watch the road, not the dashboard.
